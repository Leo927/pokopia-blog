## Overview

Resource management is the beating heart of Pokémon Pokopia. Unlike mainline Pokémon games, your progression here isn't gated by battles — it's gated by materials. Every habitat, building, and legendary unlock chain demands specific resources, and knowing how to farm them efficiently separates players who breeze through from players who spend hours grinding the wrong thing.

This guide covers the resource pipeline from raw materials to endgame Pokémetal, with practical tips for every stage.

## Understanding the Resource Pipeline

Pokopia's crafting works in layers:

1. **Raw materials** — gathered from the world (Stone, Small Logs, Squishy Clay, Ore)
2. **Processed materials** — created by Pokémon with specialties (Lumber, Brick, Ingots, Concrete)
3. **Endgame materials** — rare drops and multi-step processing (Pokémetal, Crystal Fragments, Ice)

The key insight: **your bottleneck is almost always processing, not gathering.** Raw materials are everywhere. What limits you is having the right Pokémon assigned to the right specialties.

## Priority Resources by Game Phase

### Early Game (Withered Wasteland + Bleak Beach)

Focus on these first:

- **Stone** — abundant everywhere, used in almost every recipe. Just pick it up as you explore.
- **Small Logs → Lumber** — you need a Chop Pokémon (Scyther, Pinsir, or Heracross). 10 Small Logs yield 50 Lumber. Get Scyther early — it's one of the most impactful befriends in the game.
- **Twine** — deceptively important. Cut cloth piles and cobwebs in abandoned buildings. Better yet, befriend Spinarak early for passive Twine production via its Litter specialty.
- **Squishy Clay → Brick** — break clay blocks at Bleak Beach with Rock Smash. Give clay to any Burn specialist (Torchic, Flareon, Charmander). 10 Clay → 20 Bricks.

**Pro tip:** Build a Storage Box next to your Workbench immediately. Being able to access stored materials while crafting saves enormous time.

### Mid Game (Rocky Ridges + Environment Level 3-5)

Rocky Ridges is where your resource game levels up:

- **Copper/Iron/Gold Ore** — mine ore blocks in the ridges. Process into ingots via Burn Pokémon.
- **Pokémetal Fragments** — start appearing in Withered Wasteland and Sparkling Skylands. Smelt at a Furnace to create Pokémetal Ingots. You will need *a lot* of these for endgame.
- **Volcanic Ash** — found in volcanic areas, processes into Glass at a Smelting Furnace.
- **Wheat** — needed for cooking and the Team Initiation Challenge. Find the tattered farmstead in Rocky Ridges, water the dry wheat, and start growing your own with the Rototiller move.

### Late Game (Sparkling Skylands + Palette Town)

- **Limestone → Concrete** — Sparkling Skylands has literally thousands of Limestone blocks. Process with Crush Pokémon (Conkeldurr, Onix, Steelix, Tyranitar).
- **Wastepaper → Paper** — scattered around Sparkling Skylands. Process with Recycle Pokémon (Trubbish, Porygon, Cinccino).
- **Rare Pokémetal** — the ultimate endgame material. Required for crafting the Tidal Bell and Clear Bell to summon Lugia and Ho-Oh. Farm Pokémetal Fragments aggressively.
- **Crystal Fragments** — break large crystals in Rocky Ridges mines. Needed for legendary bird habitats (Articuno's Freezing Chambers requires 10).

## The Specialty Pipeline: Your Secret Weapon

Every befriended Pokémon has one or two specialties out of 31 types. Here's what matters most:

| Specialty | What It Does | Key Pokémon |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Chop | Logs → Lumber | Scyther, Pinsir, Heracross |
| Burn | Clay → Brick, Ore → Ingots | Torchic, Flareon, Arcanine, Charmander |
| Crush | Limestone → Concrete | Conkeldurr, Onix, Golem, Tyranitar |
| Recycle | Garbage → Ore, Wastepaper → Paper | Trubbish, Porygon, Cinccino |
| Litter | Passive material drops | Spinarak (Twine), Mareep (Fluff), Wooper-Paldea (Clay) |
| Gather Honey | Passive Honey production | Combee |

**Critical fact:** Specialties have no tier system. Charmander with Burn processes ore at the exact same speed as Charizard. Don't waste time chasing evolved forms for crafting — pick whichever Pokémon you befriend first.

**Scaling tip:** If you have multiple Pokémon with the same specialty, assign them all to process simultaneously. Three Burn Pokémon smelting ore is 3x faster than one.

## Farming Locations Cheat Sheet

### Glowing Stones

The best farming spot is the **Volcano area in Rocky Ridges**. Transform into Graveler for stronger Rock Smash to break glowing deposits efficiently. Use powered-up Rock Smash on the deposits — regular hits won't work.

### Pokémetal

Two primary methods:
1. **Wild farming** — break ore blocks in Withered Wasteland and Sparkling Skylands
2. **Dream Islands** — certain Dream Islands contain Pokémetal deposits. Visit regularly.

You can also find Pokémetal Ingots and Rare Pokémetal as direct drops or shop purchases, but farming Fragments and smelting them is the most reliable method.

### Ice

Surprisingly, Ice is one of the rarest materials in Pokopia despite sounding mundane. You need it for Articuno's Freezing Chambers (50 Ice!). Farm it in cold areas and Dream Islands that feature icy terrain.

### Twine (Passive Farm)

Build Spinarak's habitat as early as possible. It drops Twine automatically near its habitat via Litter. Combine this with cutting cloth piles on Dream Islands for bulk collection.

## Dream Islands: Your Resource Multiplier

Dream Islands (accessed via Drifloon's specialty) are the single best resource source in the game. Each island has unique materials, furniture, and dolls that can't be found in main areas. Islands rotate on a schedule, so:

- **Check back daily** — island rotation means different resources each visit
- **Prioritize the island with your current bottleneck resource**
- **Collect dolls** — these unlock legendary encounters (see our Rare Pokémon guide)
- **Farm Twine, Sea Glass, and Seashells** on Ocean Dream Islands via Pikachu Doll

## Endgame Optimization Tips

1. **Stamina management matters.** PP (Power Points) is your stamina. Eat cooked food from Chef Dente for better PP restoration than raw berries. Once you build a Pokémon Center (Environment Level 3), use its Healing Station daily.

2. **Invest in PP Up and Packing Tips first.** When spending Life Coins, these two upgrades solve 90% of early-game friction. More stamina + more inventory space = longer farming sessions.

3. **Environment Level 3 is the magic number.** It unlocks the Pokémon Center kit in each area. Rush this milestone by raising Pokémon Comfort Levels — fulfill their desires, give daily gifts, and match habitat preferences (Warm, Humid, or Dark).

4. **Set up AFK resource chains.** Place Litter-specialty Pokémon in their habitats and let them passively produce materials while you focus on other tasks. Spinarak (Twine), Mareep (Fluff), and Paldean Wooper (Clay) are the MVP passive producers.

5. **Stockpile before big projects.** Before starting legendary building kits, check the full material list. Zapdos's Abandoned Power Plant needs 50 Copper Ingots, 50 Gold Ingots, 50 Sea Glass Fragments, 50 Brick, and 10 Pokémetal. Pre-farm everything to avoid mid-project frustration.

## Closing Thoughts

Resource farming in Pokopia isn't about grinding one spot for hours — it's about building an efficient pipeline. Match the right Pokémon to the right specialty, visit Dream Islands regularly, and always be passively producing materials in the background. Do this, and the endgame opens up fast.
