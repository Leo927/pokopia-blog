## Overview

Rain teams have been a staple of competitive Pokémon since Generation V. With Pelipper providing automatic Drizzle and Swift Swim sweepers hitting like trucks, rain remains one of the most consistent weather archetypes.

## Core Components

### Rain Setter: Pelipper

Pelipper is the undisputed king of rain setting in VGC. Its Drizzle ability activates rain on switch-in, and it provides valuable support moves.

```
Pelipper @ Focus Sash
Ability: Drizzle
EVs: 4 HP / 252 SpA / 252 Spe
Timid Nature
- Weather Ball
- Hurricane
- Tailwind
- Protect
```

### Swift Swim Sweeper: Barraskewda

Once rain is up, Barraskewda becomes one of the fastest Pokémon on the field.

```
Barraskewda @ Mystic Water
Ability: Swift Swim
EVs: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Adamant Nature
- Liquidation
- Close Combat
- Aqua Jet
- Protect
```

## Game Plan

1. **Lead Pelipper + Sweeper** — Set rain immediately and apply pressure
2. **Tailwind as backup** — If rain gets overwritten, Tailwind keeps you fast
3. **Protect aggressively** — Stall out opposing weather turns
4. **Switch intelligently** — Re-trigger Drizzle when needed

## Common Threats

| Threat | Solution |
|--------|----------|
| Torkoal (Sun) | Keep Pelipper healthy for re-sets |
| Grass types | Hurricane from Pelipper |
| Lightning Rod users | Target the partner instead |

## Closing Thoughts

Rain rewards aggressive play and good positioning. Practice your endgame scenarios — knowing when to sack Pelipper vs. keep it alive is the difference between good and great rain players.
